# DLOS — Positioning

## What This Is

The Digital Lineage Object Standard (DLOS) defines a self-verifying
artifact model for digital content.

It establishes a missing layer of the internet:
an object layer where artifacts carry identity, integrity, and lineage
independent of platforms.

---

## What Problem This Solves

The internet does not have a native object model.

Digital artifacts today:

- lose authorship
- lose provenance
- break across platforms
- fragment over time

Every major system rebuilds identity and ownership internally.

This creates:

- inefficiency
- duplication
- loss of history
- platform dependency

---

## What Changes

DLOS introduces:

- self-verifying artifacts
- intrinsic identity binding
- portable lineage
- platform-independent verification

This allows digital objects to persist across systems while retaining
meaning and history.

---

## Why This Matters Now

Several shifts are converging:

- AI-generated content explosion
- long-term digital preservation needs
- cross-platform interoperability demands
- increasing importance of provenance

Without an object layer, these systems remain fragmented.

DLOS addresses this gap directly.

---

## Scope

DLOS is not:

- a platform
- a marketplace
- a blockchain replacement

DLOS is a **protocol layer**.

It can be implemented by:

- archives
- research institutions
- cultural organizations
- developers
- infrastructure providers
