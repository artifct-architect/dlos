## Statement of Authorship

I, Christopher Jamar Prater, am the original architect and conceptual author of the object architecture underlying the DLC Standard.

This architecture defines a self-authenticating digital artifact structure designed to preserve authorship, integrity, and lineage of digital content independent of centralized servers or distributed ledger consensus.

The DLC Standard defines structural rules for:

- intrinsic identity binding  
- cryptographic integrity verification  
- lineage referencing  
- offline authenticity validation  

This record serves as a declaration of conceptual authorship and foundational design contribution as of the date above.

---

## Historical Development

The conceptual foundations of this work date back to **2018**, with earlier exploratory thinking preceding that period.

During development the architecture underwent several iterations and naming revisions as the design matured, including:

- Verifiable Memory Capsule
- Verifiable Lineage Encapsulation (VLE)
- Capsule Architecture
- Digital Lineage Capsule (DLC) Standard
- Artifct ecosystem

These iterations represent stages in the refinement of the underlying object architecture and protocol design.
