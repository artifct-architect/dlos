# A Living Example — Capsules in Everyday Life

## Introduction

Digital Lineage Objects (DLOS) are designed to allow digital artifacts
to carry their identity, integrity, and lineage across time.

While the protocol itself focuses on structure and verification,
its implications become easier to understand when imagined in the
context of everyday life.

The following narrative illustrates a possible scenario in which
capsules are used by creators, institutions, and communities.

---

# The Origin Capsule

A musician named Amara records a song in her home studio.

When the recording is finished, she seals the master recording
into a capsule.

The capsule contains:

- the audio file
- a cryptographic signature
- the creation timestamp
- Amara’s identity capsule reference
- descriptive metadata about the work

The capsule becomes the **origin artifact**.

It can be verified anywhere without needing a platform
or registry.

Amara shares the capsule publicly.

The file moves through the internet like any other file,
but unlike ordinary files, it retains its identity.

---

# The First Derivative

Months later, a filmmaker discovers Amara’s recording
and uses part of it in a short documentary.

The filmmaker creates a new capsule.

Inside the new capsule:

- the documentary video
- a reference to Amara’s capsule as the parent artifact
- the filmmaker’s own identity capsule
- new metadata describing the derivative work

The documentary capsule now becomes part of the same
lineage chain.

Amara Song Capsule
│
▼
Documentary Capsule


The relationship between the two works is durable and
verifiable.

---

# The Cultural Extension

A teacher later discovers the documentary and uses it
in a university course about music and storytelling.

The lecture recording becomes another capsule.

Amara Song Capsule
│
▼
Documentary Capsule
│
▼
Lecture Capsule


Each artifact acknowledges the previous work that
made it possible.

The chain continues growing.

---

# The Economic Event

Years later, the documentary is licensed for use in a
streaming platform.

A revenue event occurs.

A system interpreting capsule lineage recognizes the
relationship between the documentary and the original
song capsule.

Part of the value associated with the event flows
through the lineage chain.

Conceptually:

Streaming Revenue Event
│
▼
Documentary Capsule
│
▼
Song Capsule


The original creator remains visible within the chain
of work that followed.

---

# The Creator Domain

The creator of each capsule retains control over how
their portion of value is allocated.

Amara decides that a portion of her creator remainder
should support a community music program in her city.

She records this allocation within her capsule.

The capsule now carries not only the work itself,
but also its long-term intent.

---

# Institutional Stewardship

A digital music archive later acquires the song capsule
for preservation.

The archive does not replace the artifact.

Instead, it becomes a **steward of the capsule**.

The capsule continues to exist independently,
but the archive helps ensure its long-term
availability and cultural context.

The artifact now lives simultaneously:

- in personal collections
- within educational materials
- in cultural archives
- across digital networks

---

# Decades Later

Many years pass.

The song has inspired documentaries,
performances, and research papers.

All of these works exist as capsules
connected through lineage.

Someone discovering the lecture capsule
can still trace its ancestry back through
the documentary to the original recording.

Even if platforms have disappeared,
the artifacts still verify themselves.

The chain remains intact.

---

# What This Example Shows

Capsules transform digital artifacts from isolated files
into connected cultural objects.

Through lineage references, artifacts can form long-lived
chains of influence.

These chains allow systems to recognize:

- authorship
- derivation
- cultural contribution
- historical continuity

In this way, capsules support a durable record of how
creative and intellectual work evolves over time.

---

# Summary

Digital Lineage Objects allow artifacts to retain identity,
integrity, and ancestry across systems and generations.

When many such artifacts exist, they form a network of
relationships that preserves cultural memory.

This example illustrates how capsules can quietly integrate
into everyday creative, educational, and archival workflows
while maintaining a verifiable connection to their origins.
