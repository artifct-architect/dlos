## Statement of Authorship

I, Christopher Jamar Prater, am the original architect and conceptual author of the object architecture underlying the Digital Lineage Object Standard (DLOS).

This architecture defines a self-verifying digital artifact structure designed to preserve authorship, integrity, and lineage of digital content independent of centralized servers, registries, or distributed ledger systems.

The DLOS object architecture establishes structural rules for:

- intrinsic identity binding  
- cryptographic integrity verification  
- lineage referencing  
- offline authenticity validation  

This record serves as a declaration of authorship and foundational design contribution as of the date above.

---

## Historical Development

The conceptual foundations of this work date back to **2018**, with exploratory thinking preceding that period.

During development the architecture underwent multiple iterations and internal naming revisions as the design matured.

Some early working names and conceptual labels were used during the research and prototyping phases of development. These names are not used within the public protocol specification.

For the purposes of the public standard and reference implementation, the architecture is referred to as the **Digital Lineage Object Standard (DLOS)**.
