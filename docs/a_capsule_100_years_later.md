# A Capsule 100 Years Later

## Introduction

Digital systems change rapidly.

Platforms rise and fall.
File formats become obsolete.
Servers disappear.
Companies dissolve.

Yet human culture, knowledge, and creativity extend across
generations.

Digital Lineage Objects (DLOS) were designed with this
longer horizon in mind.

The following scenario imagines what it might look like
to encounter a capsule artifact one hundred years after
its creation.

---

# The Discovery

In the year 2126, a student researching early
twenty-first century music opens a digital archive.

Among the materials is a file labeled:

amara_song_master.cap


Unlike many other files from that era, the artifact
opens without relying on any specific platform.

The capsule verifies itself.

Inside the capsule are:

- the original audio recording
- the creator’s identity reference
- the creation timestamp
- a cryptographic signature
- the capsule’s lineage metadata

The student knows immediately:

- who created the artifact
- when it was created
- that it has not been altered
- and that it belongs to a chain of related works.

---

# Institutional Stewardship

Over the decades, several institutions
have preserved copies of the capsule.

These include:

- a national archive
- a university library
- a cultural heritage foundation

Each institution hosts the capsule but does not
control it.

The artifact’s identity and integrity remain
independent of any single repository.

The capsule continues to verify even if one
archive disappears.

---

# Economic Lineage

The capsule viewer also shows that the
original work participated in many derivative
projects over the decades.

Because those projects referenced the
original artifact, the lineage remained visible.

When certain derivative works generated value,
a portion of that value flowed through the
artifact’s lineage.

The economic events are recorded as
verifiable entries attached to the
capsule’s history.

Even after a century, the record shows how
the work contributed to later creations.

---

# Cultural Continuity

What began as a simple home recording
has become part of a much larger cultural
network.

The artifact now appears in:

- music history research
- educational lectures
- archival exhibitions
- documentary retrospectives

The capsule allows each of these artifacts
to remain connected to the work that
preceded them.

The lineage chain tells a story that would
otherwise have been lost.

---

# Why the Capsule Still Works

The student notices something remarkable.

The capsule does not depend on any
particular platform.

It does not require the streaming services
that once distributed the song.

It does not rely on the company that
originally hosted it.

The capsule remains interpretable because
it carries its own structure:

- identity
- signature
- lineage reference
- verifiable metadata

Even a century later, the artifact can
still be understood.

---

# A Durable Cultural Record

The student realizes that this capsule is
not just a file.

It is part of a historical chain of work.

Each capsule in the lineage preserves
a small piece of the story.

Together they form a durable record of
how ideas, art, and knowledge evolved
over time.

---

# Summary

Digital Lineage Objects allow artifacts
to carry identity, integrity, and lineage
independent of the platforms that once
distributed them.

Because lineage relationships remain
embedded in the artifacts themselves,
capsules can preserve cultural context
across generations.

A capsule created today may still be
verifiable, understandable, and connected
to its descendants a century from now.
