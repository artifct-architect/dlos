# Dedication

This work is dedicated to those who continue to imagine a better future for humanity.

To those who share my lineage and heritage:

You are bold, beautiful, and brilliant.

As an African American with a vision, I have often experienced what many before me have known — being disregarded, underestimated, or overlooked within the society in which I live. Yet none of those things define our worth, our intelligence, or our capacity to create.

Ideas can emerge from anywhere. Vision can come from any place, any background, any community.

This work was created with a belief that knowledge, creativity, and human memory belong to all people — and that the preservation of our shared history is a responsibility that transcends borders, systems, and generations.

I also dedicate this work to my family.

To my mother, who instilled my character, gave me life, and raised me.

And to my younger brother — born blind and without sight — who has always seen the good in me even when others would not. His belief in me never wavered, and he actively supported the decisions I chose to make.
To those who may one day encounter this work and wonder whether their voice matters:

Know that it does.

Wherever you come from, and whatever challenges you face, hold onto your curiosity, your creativity, and your dignity.

You are never lesser.
